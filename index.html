Product Overview & Identity
Chemical Identity

2-Fucosyllactose (2'-FL) is a naturally occurring trisaccharide and the most abundant Human Milk Oligosaccharide (HMO) in human breast milk, constituting 20–35% of all HMOs. Structurally it consists of L-fucose linked α-1,2 to the galactose unit of lactose, forming a three-sugar molecule: fucose-galactose-glucose. It is nutritionally indigestible, acting as a prebiotic that selectively feeds beneficial gut microbiota (especially Bifidobacterium) while acting as a receptor decoy blocking pathogen adhesion to intestinal walls. It is commercially produced via microbial fermentation using engineered E. coli or Corynebacterium glutamicum strains. FSSAI approved 2-FL for infant formula in India in September 2024, marking the first HMO approval in India.

Synthesis & Manufacturing
ℹ️ All three commercial synthesis routes for 2'-FL have been demonstrated at industrial scale. Microbial fermentation using engineered E. coli is the dominant commercial route worldwide, accounting for ~85% of production capacity. Patent landscape is active — key patents held by Jennewein (acquired by Abbott/Chr. Hansen), Glycom (acquired by DSM), and Kyowa Hakko Bio.

Applications, End-Products & Market Sizes
Application 1: Infant Formula — Core & Largest Market

2'-FL is the most prevalent HMO in human breast milk and its primary commercial use is as an additive to infant formula to replicate the immunological benefits of breastfeeding. It selectively promotes Bifidobacterium growth, reduces pathogen adhesion, decreases incidence of diarrhea/infections, and supports immune system development. Infant formula remains the dominant application segment, accounting for ~60–69% of total HMO demand by value.

Application 2: Dietary Supplements & Functional Foods

Beyond infant nutrition, 2'-FL is gaining traction in adult gut health supplements, immunity boosters, and functional foods. Clinical evidence supports benefits in IBS, gut microbiome modulation, anti-aging (via antioxidant pathway regulation in d-galactose models), and norovirus inhibition. Post-COVID immune health awareness accelerated consumer interest in HMO supplements.

Application 3: Pharmaceutical / Clinical Research & Reference Standards

2'-FL is used as a reference standard in pharmaceutical analytical labs (USP compendium), as a tool compound in gut microbiome research, and is being investigated for therapeutic roles in conditions like necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC), IBD, norovirus infection prevention, and short bowel syndrome. CRO and clinical trial demand supports steady small-volume high-purity trade.
